Local Food:

Culinary Delights of Yogyakarta

Explore delicious photos of iconic Jogja dishes

Gudeg

Young jackfruit cooked in coconut milk, served with chicken and krecek. Sweet-savory, perfect for Jogja breakfast.

Bakpia Pathok

Crispy pastry filled with mung bean paste. A must-have souvenir from Yogyakarta.

Sate Klathak

Goat satay skewered with iron sticks, grilled simply without oil. Naturally savory from Bantul.

Wedang Ronde

Warm ginger drink with glutinous rice balls and palm fruit. Perfect for a chilly night at an angkringan.

Ayam Goreng Kalasan

Fried free-range chicken with sweet and spicy coconut milk seasoning. Crispy and juicy with fresh vegetables.

Kipo

Green pandan coconut snacks wrapped in banana leaves. Sweet and refreshing, a specialty of Kotagede.

Geplak

Brown sugar and grated coconut cakes, shaped into balls. A simple traditional sweet from Bantul.

Mie Lethek

Traditional noodles made from cassava leaves, served in clear chicken broth. Unique and healthy.

Brongkos

A Yogyakarta specialty stew made of black-eyed peas in coconut milk with spices and kluwek.

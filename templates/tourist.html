Tourist Places:

Yogyakarta's Iconic Tourism

Explore the iconic places of Yogyakarta

Prambanan Temple

The largest Hindu temple in Indonesia, built in the 9th century. A UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning Ramayana reliefs.

Yogyakarta Palace

The palace of Sultan Hamengkubuwono, a center of Javanese culture since 1755. Explore the museum, gardens, and traditional ceremonies.

Malioboro

An iconic street in the city center, filled with street vendors and batik. Enjoy street food, shopping, and night parades.

Parangtritis Beach

A beautiful beach with big waves and the myth of Nyi Roro Kidul. Activities: ATV, paragliding, and mesmerizing sunsets.

Taman Sari

An 18th-century water palace complex with secret tunnels. A mix of Javanese, Portuguese, and Hindu architecture.

Kaliurang

A tourist village on the slopes of Mount Merapi, with cool air and flower gardens. Light trekking and volcanic mountain views.

Jomblang Cave

A vertical cave with midday sunlight (heavenly light). Caving adventures and karst nature in Gunungkidul.

Affandi Museum

The home of painter Affandi with a collection of expressionist paintings. Unique architecture resembling a Javanese stilt house.

Alun-Alun Kidul

A city square with the masangin tradition (walking blindfolded between banyan trees). Nighttime is filled with lanterns and horse-drawn carriages.

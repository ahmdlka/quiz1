Yogyakarta

A special region with a rich heritage of culture, history, and enduring beauty.

History

Yogyakarta emerged as a center of power since the establishment of the Yogyakarta Sultanate in 1755. The city is known as a symbol of the Indonesian people's struggle, especially when it served as the temporary capital of Indonesia during the independence revolution from 1946â€“1949.

Culture

Yogyakarta is renowned for its strong Javanese culture, ranging from palace traditions, batik, gamelan, to wayang kulit. Local wisdom and Javanese philosophy are well-preserved in the daily lives of its people.

Culinary

Gudeg is Yogyakarta's most famous traditional dish, made from young jackfruit cooked in coconut milk. Additionally, there are bakpia, sate klathak, and wedang ronde, which are favorites among tourists.

Tourism

Yogyakarta offers a variety of tourist destinations, from the Yogyakarta Palace, Prambanan Temple, Malioboro, to natural attractions such as Kaliurang and Parangtritis Beach. The city is one of Indonesia's top tourist destinations.
